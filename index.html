The Lord's Prayer

Our Father, which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy Name. Thy Kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, As it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread. And forgive us our trespasses, As we forgive them that trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation, But deliver us from evil. For thine is the kingdom, The power, and the glory, For ever and ever. Amen.

What Our Father Prayer Teaches us

Honor God
Recognize God's holiness and power
Seek God's will
Surrender to God's plans and purposes
Ask for provision
Request daily bread and necessities
Seek forgiveness
Ask for pardon for sins and mistakes
Forgive others
Let go of grudges and resentments
Seek protection
Ask for guidance and protection from evil

How to pray

  • Find a Quiet Place: Seek a comfortable and peaceful location where you can focus and have a sense of solitude.
  • Begin with Reverence: Start your prayer with a sense of respect and awareness of God's presence.
  • Adoration and Praise: Begin by praising God for His greatness, goodness, and love.
  • Confession: Acknowledge your sins and ask for forgiveness.
  • Thanksgiving: Express gratitude for God's grace, love, and blessings in your life.
  • Supplication (Petition): Present your requests and concerns to God.
  • Intercession: Pray for others, such as family, friends, the community, and the world.
